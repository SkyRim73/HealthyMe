üìã Overview & Plan Philosophy

Welcome to your interactive Keralite Calisthenics Blueprint. This plan is designed for individuals with prior gym experience, focusing on building functional strength and flexibility using bodyweight exercises. Calisthenics is adaptable, requiring minimal equipment, making it perfect for home training. This guide emphasizes a holistic approach, integrating structured workouts, recovery, nutrition, and motivation.

Core Principles:

  • PPLPPLR Split: Push-Pull-Legs, repeated twice, with one rest/active recovery day. This ensures balanced muscle development and adequate recovery.
  • Workout Duration: Each session is designed for 30-60 minutes (including warm-up/cool-down).
  • Intensity (RPE): Aim for a Rate of Perceived Exertion of 7-8 (out of 10) for most strength exercises, meaning you feel you could do 2-3 more reps with good form.

üî• Warm-Up Protocol (5-10 minutes)

A thorough warm-up prepares your body for exercise, reduces injury risk, and enhances performance. Focus on dynamic movements.

A. Light Cardiovascular Activity (2-3 minutes):

  • Jumping Jacks: 30-60 seconds.
  • High Knees: 30-60 seconds.
  • Butt Kicks: 30-60 seconds.

B. Dynamic Stretches & Mobility Drills (3-7 minutes):

  • Arm Circles (Forward & Backward): 10-15 reps each direction.
  • Leg Swings (Forward/Backward & Side-to-Side): 10-12 controlled swings per leg, each direction.
  • Torso Twists: 15-20 reps.
  • Hip Circles: 10-15 reps each direction.
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: 8-10 reps.
  • Quadruped Thoracic Rotations: 8-10 reps per side.
  • (Optional) Wrist Circles/Flexion-Extension: 10-15 reps.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cool-Down & Flexibility Routine (5-10 minutes)

Cooling down helps your body transition back to a resting state and improves flexibility. Hold static stretches for 20-30 seconds each.

A. Light Activity (1-2 minutes):

  • Slow walking in place.
  • Gentle arm and leg shakes.

B. Static Stretches (4-8 minutes):

  • Overhead Triceps Stretch
  • Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch
  • Doorway Chest Stretch
  • Standing Quadriceps Stretch
  • Standing or Seated Hamstring Stretch
  • Standing Calf Stretch
  • Knee-to-Chest Pose
  • Child's Pose
  • Seated Spinal Twist
  • Butterfly Stretch

üßò Rest & Active Recovery (Day 7)

Day 7 is crucial for recovery. Active recovery promotes blood flow, reduces soreness, and maintains mobility without stressing the body.

Recommended Active Recovery Options (20-45 minutes):

  • Yoga (Highly Recommended): Focus on gentle flows, restorative poses, and mindful breathing.
    • Styles: Hatha, gentle Viniyasa, Yin, or Restorative Yoga.
    • Example Poses: Downward-Facing Dog, Cat-Cow, Child's Pose, Pigeon Pose (modified), Supine Spinal Twist, Legs Up The Wall, Savasana.
    • Resources: Search "Yoga With Adriene" or "Boho Beautiful" on YouTube.
  • Light Walking or Cycling: 20-30 minutes at a comfortable pace.
  • Foam Rolling: Self-myofascial release for tight muscles.
  • Full-Body Static Stretching: A longer session of the cool-down stretches.

Listen to Your Body: If feeling excessively fatigued or in pain, prioritize full rest or very light activity.

üìà Progression Guide

Progressive overload is key to continuous improvement. Gradually increase the challenge over time by manipulating exercises.

üìà Increase Repetitions or Sets

Once you hit the upper rep range with good form, add more reps or an extra set.

ü§∏ More Challenging Variations

Alter body position or leverage (e.g., Decline Push-ups, Pistol Squat progressions).

‚è±Ô∏è Decrease Rest Time

Reduce rest between sets to increase workout density (e.g., from 90s to 60s).

üí™ Increase Time Under Tension (TuT)

Slow down the lowering phase (eccentric) or add pauses at challenging points.

Guidance on Progression:

  • Focus on one variable at a time for a given exercise.
  • Form First: Never compromise form for more reps or harder variations.
  • Listen to Your Body: Adjust based on how you feel; don't push through pain.
  • Patience is Key: Significant gains take time and consistency.
